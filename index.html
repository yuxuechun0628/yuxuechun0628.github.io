About

I am an urban planner & researcher based in Shenzhen, China, preparing for Fall 2027 Ph.D. applications in urban planning. I hold a master's degree from the University of Hong Kong and have professional experience at the China Academy of Urban Planning and Design.

My research examines how urban transformation — particularly gentrification — reshapes everyday spaces and the populations who depend on them. I combine spatial analysis, computational text methods, and theoretical frameworks from environmental gerontology and ecological psychology to study the distributional consequences of neighborhood change for older adults and children.

Research

Gentrification and Healthy Aging Resources

How does neighborhood gentrification restructure access to healthy aging resources? I examine ~2,100 NYC census tracts across three resource domains (physical-activity, cognitive-stimulation, aging-in-place) using spatial regression (MGWR) and XGBoost-SHAP, drawing on Lawton & Nahemow's press-competence model and the cognability framework.

Children's Park Affordance Actualization

Applying Kyttä's affordance actualization framework across Bay Area and Shenzhen parks, I use UGC/NLP methods to measure breadth, intensity, and quality of actualized affordances. This work introduces "caregiver support affordance" to capture the dual-dimension supply structure specific to young children's park use.

Gentrification and the Semantic Shift of Urban Parks

How does gentrification transform collective place meanings of urban parks? Using a quasi-experimental matched design and semantic analysis of park UGC, this project bridges Stedman's place meaning framework with the social constructionist tradition (Cresswell, Massey).
